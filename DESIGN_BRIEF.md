## Phase 3 — Copy

### Hero (Style E — Alternating headlines)

- **Tag pill:** Music Lessons in Anniston, AL
- **Line 1 (white):** Find Your
- **Line 2 (gold, italic):** Voice
- **Line 3 (periwinkle):** in Music
- **Sub-headline:** Singing, storytelling, and movement lessons that help you connect with music — no experience needed. Just bring yourself.
- **Primary CTA:** Book a Discovery Session (gold button, links to contact.html or Calendly)
- **Secondary CTA:** Learn More (ghost button, links to #services or services.html)

### Problem Section (optional on homepage — can integrate into About page)

- **Headline:** Music Shouldn't Feel Like a Test You're Afraid to Fail
- **Body:** Most of us love music, but many feel like we aren't "musical enough" to participate in it. Whether it's the memory of a rigid classroom or the fear of hitting a wrong note, intimidation can keep you silent. At KRB Studio, we believe that everyone has a story to tell and a song to sing. You shouldn't have to be a prodigy to experience the emotional relief and confidence that music provides.

### Services (3 cards with hover accent bar)

1. **Singing Lessons**
   Personalized vocal coaching focused on confidence and the joy of music — not perfection.

2. **Musical Storytelling**
   Combine lyrics, rhythm, and narrative to find meaningful creative expression through song.

3. **Movement & Rhythm**
   Whole-body movement and dance that makes music a physical experience, not just academic.

- **Section eyebrow:** What We Offer
- **Section headline:** Three Paths to Finding Your Voice

### How It Works (Vertical timeline)

- **Section headline:** Your Path to Musical Confidence

1. **The Discovery Session**
   We'll meet at the Greenbriar Studio to talk about your interests and what's held you back. No pressure, no auditions — just a conversation.

2. **A Personalized Plan**
   Katie will design a custom approach that blends singing, movement, or storytelling based on your specific goals and comfort level.

3. **Find Your Sound**
   Begin a rewarding journey of self-discovery where the focus is on growth and joy — not technical perfection. Your voice, your story.

### Why Us / Guide Section (About page focus)

- **Headline:** Meet Your Guide, Katie Burns
- **Body:** With years of experience teaching woodwinds, theory, and movement across the globe, Katie Burns brings a unique, multi-disciplinary approach to Anniston. As a staff member of CAST Kidz and CAPA, she is deeply embedded in the local arts community and understands the transformative power of performance. Based in the professional environment of the Greenbriar Dear Entrepreneurial Center, Katie provides a sanctuary for students of all ages who are ready to discover their musical interests and passions.

### Testimonial (Large featured quote, style D)

- **Featured quote:** "Always wanted to learn to play the saxophone but never thought I could. Thanks to Kathryn I'm now enjoying playing... My lessons are fun and have given me so much confidence."
- **Attribution:** Adult Student, KRB Studio

Additional quotes for rotation / services page:
- "Kathryn is an excellent teacher: patient, very knowledgeable with the ability to encourage and assist the pupil to achieve attainable goals."
- "He's looking forward to more lessons! Very clear instructions and very patient. Highly recommend."

### FAQ (4 questions)

1. **Q:** I have no musical background. Can I still join?
   **A:** Absolutely. We specialize in working with beginners and those who feel intimidated by traditional music education.

2. **Q:** What exactly is "Movement through Music"?
   **A:** It is a holistic approach that uses the body to understand rhythm and expression, making music a physical and emotional experience rather than just an academic one.

3. **Q:** Are lessons online or in-person?
   **A:** We offer in-person sessions at the Greenbriar Dear Entrepreneurial Center in Anniston, as well as online options for those who prefer the comfort of home.

4. **Q:** Is there an age limit?
   **A:** We teach all ages, from young children to adults. It is never too late — or too early — to start your musical journey.
